# Why Every Business Needs a Data Strategy

I often get called in to fix technical problems -- a slow dashboard, a broken pipeline, a messy data model. But more often than not, the root cause is not technical. It is the absence of a data strategy.

## What is a Data Strategy?

A data strategy is a plan that defines how your organization collects, stores, manages, and uses data to achieve its business goals. It is not a 100-page document that sits on a shelf. A good data strategy is a living framework that guides daily decisions.

It typically covers:

- **Data architecture** -- Where does data live and how does it flow?
- **Governance** -- Who owns the data? Who can access it? How is quality ensured?
- **Technology choices** -- Which tools and platforms support your goals?
- **People and skills** -- Does your team have the capabilities needed?
- **Use cases** -- What business problems are you solving with data?

## The Cost of Not Having One

Without a strategy, organizations tend to accumulate what I call "accidental architecture." Each team solves their own data problems independently, leading to:

- **Data silos** -- The same data exists in five different places, none of which agree
- **Tool sprawl** -- Every department has their own reporting tool, none integrated
- **Duplicated effort** -- Multiple teams building the same reports from scratch
- **Security gaps** -- No consistent approach to access control or compliance
- **Technical debt** -- Quick fixes that become permanent, making future changes harder

The financial cost is real. I have seen companies spend more on maintaining their accidental architecture than it would cost to build a proper platform from scratch.

## Starting Small

You do not need a six-month consulting engagement to create a data strategy. Start with three questions:

1. **What decisions do we need data to support?** -- This grounds everything in business value
2. **Where does our data currently live?** -- Map your existing landscape honestly
3. **What is causing the most pain today?** -- This tells you where to invest first

From there, you can build out a roadmap that prioritizes quick wins while laying the foundation for long-term scalability.

## When to Bring in Help

If your organization is about to make a significant platform investment -- migrating to the cloud, adopting Microsoft Fabric, or consolidating reporting tools -- that is the ideal time to define or refine your data strategy. The technology decisions should follow from the strategy, not the other way around.
